**First appeared:** (date of first entry referencing this)
**Last updated:** (date)

## Definition
The core identity wound expressed as: "If I am ordinary, I am worthless." It manifests as compulsive overachievement, inability to rest without guilt, need to be the best in the room, avoidance of anything where failure is public, and a chronic inability to receive love that is not conditional on performance. Current work involves recognizing when the wound is driving decisions versus when genuine standards are driving them — the two can look identical from the outside.
