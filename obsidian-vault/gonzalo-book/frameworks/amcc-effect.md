## Definition
The Anterior Mid-Cingulate Cortex effect — the neurological mechanism by which voluntary exposure to discomfort builds override capacity. When you choose to do hard things that you do not want to do, the AMCC grows and strengthens the brain's ability to override impulse, resist comfort-seeking, and sustain effort under pressure. It is the biological basis for why suffering on purpose builds something real.
